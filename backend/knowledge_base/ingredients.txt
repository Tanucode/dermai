SKINCARE INGREDIENTS KNOWLEDGE BASE
=====================================

NIACINAMIDE (Vitamin B3)
- Concentration: 5-10% for most benefits
- Benefits: Reduces dark spots and hyperpigmentation, controls excess sebum production, minimizes the appearance of pores, strengthens the skin barrier, reduces redness and blotchiness
- Best for: Oily skin, acne-prone skin, dark spots, large pores
- How to use: Apply 2-3 drops after toner, morning and night. Can be layered with most other ingredients.
- Pairs well with: Hyaluronic acid, retinol, zinc
- Avoid pairing with: High-concentration Vitamin C (can reduce efficacy)
- Products to look for: Minimalist 10% Niacinamide, The Ordinary Niacinamide 10% + Zinc 1%

HYALURONIC ACID
- Concentration: 1-2%
- Benefits: Attracts and retains up to 1000x its weight in water, deeply hydrates all skin layers, plumps skin to reduce fine lines, suitable for all skin types including oily skin
- Best for: Dry skin, dehydrated skin, fine lines, all skin types
- How to use: Apply on damp skin immediately after cleansing, before other serums. Follow with moisturizer to seal in moisture.
- Pairs well with: All ingredients
- Note: Must be applied to damp skin or it pulls moisture FROM the skin
- Products to look for: Neutrogena Hydro Boost, The Ordinary Hyaluronic Acid 2% + B5

VITAMIN C (L-Ascorbic Acid)
- Concentration: 10-20% for maximum efficacy
- Benefits: Powerful antioxidant, fades dark spots and post-acne marks, boosts collagen production, brightens dull skin, protects from UV damage (use with SPF)
- Best for: Dark spots, dull skin, anti-aging, hyperpigmentation
- How to use: Apply in the morning after cleansing. Always follow with SPF.
- Pairs well with: Vitamin E, ferulic acid (stabilizes Vitamin C), SPF
- Avoid pairing with: Niacinamide at high concentrations, benzoyl peroxide
- Note: Unstable ingredient — store in dark, cool place. If it turns orange/brown, discard.
- Products to look for: Minimalist 10% Vitamin C, Skinceuticals CE Ferulic

RETINOL (Vitamin A)
- Concentration: Start at 0.025%, build to 0.1%, then 0.3%, then 1%
- Benefits: Speeds cell turnover (removes dead cells faster), reduces wrinkles and fine lines, unclogs pores, fades dark spots, increases collagen production
- Best for: Anti-aging, acne, dark spots, textured skin
- How to use: NIGHT USE ONLY. Start 2x per week, build up slowly. Always use SPF next morning.
- Pairs well with: Hyaluronic acid, ceramides, niacinamide (buffer retinol irritation)
- Avoid pairing with: AHA/BHA same night, benzoyl peroxide, Vitamin C
- Note: Beginners will experience purging (temporary breakout). This is normal for 4-6 weeks.
- Products to look for: The Ordinary Retinol 0.2%, Paula's Choice 1% Retinol

SALICYLIC ACID (BHA)
- Concentration: 0.5-2%
- Benefits: Oil-soluble so it penetrates INTO pores, unclogs blackheads and whiteheads, anti-inflammatory for acne, exfoliates inside the pore lining
- Best for: Oily skin, acne, blackheads, whiteheads, clogged pores
- How to use: Use 2-3x per week. Can use as toner or spot treatment. Morning or night.
- Pairs well with: Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid
- Avoid pairing with: Other exfoliants (AHA) on same day
- Products to look for: Paula's Choice BHA Exfoliant, Minimalist 2% Salicylic Acid

GLYCOLIC ACID / LACTIC ACID (AHA)
- Concentration: 5-10% for daily use toners, up to 30% for peels
- Benefits: Chemical exfoliant that dissolves dead skin cells, brightens skin, improves texture, fades dark spots, stimulates collagen
- Best for: Dull skin, textured skin, dark spots, dry skin (lactic acid is gentler)
- How to use: Use 2-3x per week at night. Always follow with moisturizer and SPF next morning.
- Pairs well with: Hyaluronic acid, ceramides
- Avoid pairing with: Retinol same night, Vitamin C

CERAMIDES
- Benefits: Restores and strengthens the skin barrier (the protective layer), prevents water loss, soothes redness and irritation, protects from environmental damage
- Best for: Dry skin, sensitive skin, eczema, compromised skin barrier
- How to use: Apply as moisturizer, morning and night
- Pairs well with: ALL ingredients — ceramides help tolerate actives
- Products to look for: CeraVe Moisturizing Cream, Dr Jart Ceramidin

AZELAIC ACID
- Concentration: 10-20%
- Benefits: Treats hyperpigmentation, rosacea, and acne simultaneously, anti-inflammatory, kills acne bacteria, brightens skin, gentle enough for sensitive skin and pregnancy
- Best for: Rosacea, dark spots, sensitive skin, pregnancy-safe skincare
- How to use: Morning or night, after toner, before moisturizer
- Pairs well with: Niacinamide, hyaluronic acid

CAFFEINE
- Benefits: Constricts blood vessels to reduce puffiness and dark circles under eyes, antioxidant properties, reduces inflammation
- Best for: Dark circles, puffy eyes, eye bags
- How to use: Apply eye cream or serum with caffeine in the morning, gently pat around eye area
- Products to look for: The Ordinary Caffeine Solution 5% + EGCG, L'Oreal Revitalift Eye Serum

BENZOYL PEROXIDE
- Concentration: 2.5-5% for most people (higher is not more effective, just more irritating)
- Benefits: Kills acne bacteria (P. acnes), reduces active breakouts quickly
- Best for: Active acne, cystic acne
- How to use: Spot treatment on active pimples only. Can bleach fabric — let it dry completely.
- Caution: Very drying. Always moisturize after. Avoid eyes and mouth.

SPF / SUNSCREEN
- SPF 50+ is recommended for daily use
- The single most important anti-aging step — prevents dark spots, wrinkles, and skin cancer
- Use EVERY morning, rain or shine, indoors or outdoors (UV penetrates windows)
- Reapply every 2 hours if outdoors
- Physical (Zinc Oxide, Titanium Dioxide): Better for sensitive skin, immediate protection
- Chemical: Lighter texture, invisible, needs 15-20 min to activate
